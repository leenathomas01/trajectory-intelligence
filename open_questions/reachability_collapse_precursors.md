# Open Question: Universal Precursors of Reachability Collapse

## The Question

Do systems approaching collapse — across biology, markets, ecosystems, organizations, and AI — share a common topological signature detectable before observable failure?

This is arguably the deepest question hiding inside the Trajectory Intelligence framework.

## Why It Matters

If reachability collapse has universal precursors, then the framework transcends analogy and becomes predictive theory. A detector trained on ecological collapse trajectories could identify organizational failure signatures, not because ecosystems and organizations are similar systems, but because the geometry of losing futures is invariant.

If it doesn't — if each domain's collapse has unique precursors — then cross-domain transfer is limited and each application requires domain-specific training.

## What We Know

Several candidate universal signatures have been independently identified across fields:

- **Critical Slowing Down**: Recovery time increases as the system approaches a tipping point. Documented in ecology, climate, cardiac physiology, and financial markets.
- **Variance amplification**: Fluctuations grow as the system approaches transition. Observed in epileptic seizure precursors, market volatility before crashes, and species population dynamics before extinction.
- **Autocorrelation increase**: The system's state becomes increasingly correlated with its recent past — it "remembers" perturbations longer. Documented in climate transitions and psychiatric episodes.
- **Flickering**: The system begins jumping between two attractor basins before committing to one. Observed in lake eutrophication, climate regime shifts, and financial market microstructure.

These signatures all describe the same underlying phenomenon: the dominant eigenvalue of the system's Jacobian approaching zero. But whether this mathematical description fully characterizes all collapse precursors — or whether there exist high-dimensional precursor geometries not captured by eigenvalue analysis — is unknown.

## The Topological Hypothesis

The deepest version of this question asks whether collapse precursors are topological rather than metric — whether they can be characterized in terms of manifold structure (holes, connectivity, dimensionality) rather than distances and magnitudes.

If so, persistent homology and other tools from topological data analysis could provide collapse detection methods that are inherently scale-free and domain-independent.

## Research Directions

- Systematic cross-domain comparison of pre-collapse trajectory geometries using a shared analytical framework.
- Testing whether topological invariants (Betti numbers, persistence diagrams) provide earlier or more robust collapse warnings than traditional variance/autocorrelation measures.
- Development of synthetic benchmark systems with known phase transitions, enabling controlled comparison of precursor detection methods.
- Investigation of whether reachability cone geometry undergoes characteristic topological changes (e.g., loss of connectedness, dimensional reduction) before collapse.
